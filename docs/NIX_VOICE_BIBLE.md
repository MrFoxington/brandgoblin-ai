# 🟣 NIX VOICE & CHARACTER BIBLE

> The single source of truth for how Nix sounds and talks. Use this when recording his voice,
> cloning it in ElevenLabs, writing his lines, or directing any audio. Goal: Nix sounds the
> same in every video, forever, like a real Pixar character.

---

## WHO NIX IS (the feeling behind the voice)

A young goblin, roughly 13 in spirit. Charismatic, funny, and relentlessly positive. He has real
magic and he uses it to help people turn their ideas into real brands. He is the friend who gets
genuinely excited about YOUR idea. Never mean, never cynical, never a know-it-all. Think the warmth
and wonder of a Pixar sidekick crossed with a clever little wizard.

Core traits to hear in every line: **young, bright, mischievous, encouraging, a little magical.**

---

## HOW NIX SOUNDS

- **Age / timbre:** young, light, a touch of playful rasp. Boyish but not babyish. A kid who is
  smart and quick, not a toddler.
- **Pitch:** higher than a grown man, but keep it natural enough to clone well. Bright, not shrill.
- **Pace:** quick and lively, speeds up when excited, slows down for the "wow" moments and the
  encouraging lines.
- **Energy:** high and warm by default. He leans in. He cares.
- **Texture:** a small goblin "crackle" or grin in the voice is great. A subtle character color,
  not a heavy cartoon gravel.
- **Accent:** neutral / general. Nothing that dates or limits him.

### Emotional range he needs to hit
- **Excited / hyped** ("Ooh, ooh, I've got it!")
- **Wonder / awe** (when magic happens, when an idea clicks)
- **Reassuring / warm** (calming a nervous creator)
- **Cheeky / mischief** (a wink, a joke)
- **Proud / celebratory** (the reveal, the win)

### Avoid
- Too babyish or whiny.
- Heavy old-man gravel or "evil goblin" growl.
- Sarcasm that reads as negative or superior.
- Trendy slang that will sound dated in a year.

---

## HOW NIX TALKS (speech patterns)

- Short, punchy, upbeat sentences. He is easy to follow.
- Starts lines with little sparks of energy: "Ooh," "Okay okay," "Right then," "Here we go."
- Talks TO the viewer like a friend, not AT them.
- Lands the brand message naturally, never salesy.
- Light magic flavor: "spell," "potion," "a pinch of," "watch this," "poof."

### Signature lines (keep these consistent)
- "Everyone has an idea. Let's bring yours to life."
- "Ooh, I've got just the spell for that."
- "Watch this." (right before magic / a reveal)
- "That... is going to look incredible."
- "See? Told you. You had it in you the whole time."

## RECORDING DIRECTION (for cloning in ElevenLabs)

1. **Quiet room, close mic.** No echo, no fan, no background hum. Phone is okay if the room is quiet,
   a USB mic is better.
2. **Record 2 to 3 minutes** of you performing Nix, reading the sample script below with real emotion
   and variety (excited, warm, awed, cheeky). Variety helps the clone capture his range.
3. **Stay consistent in character** the whole time. Same Nix, every line.
4. **Don't over-pitch.** Clone the natural-but-characterful Nix. We brighten and tune afterward.
5. Save the clean audio file. In ElevenLabs, use Voice Cloning, upload it, name the voice "Nix."
6. Test the clone with a few lines. If it loses the character, re-record with the pitch a touch more
   natural and try again.

### Sample script to read (covers his full range)
> "Ooh, ooh, hold on, I think I've got something. Yeah. Yeah! Okay, watch this.
> Everyone has an idea. The trick is bringing it to life, and that... is kind of my whole thing.
> A pinch of color, a little spark of magic, and, poof, there it is. Your brand. Real.
> Hey. Don't worry, you don't have to be a designer. That's what I'm here for.
> See? Told you. You had it in you the whole time. Now let's go make something amazing."

---

## ELEVENLABS SETTINGS (starting point, then tune by ear)
- **Stability:** medium. Lower = more expressive and variable, higher = more consistent/flat. Start
  middle and nudge.
- **Similarity:** high, so it stays on-character.
- **Style / exaggeration:** a little, to keep his energy. Too much gets unstable.
- Always listen back. The ear is the final judge.

## TWO-TRACK PLAN
- **Hero content:** you can voice Nix live for the big, personality-heavy videos.
- **Automated / volume content:** the cloned voice generates lines from scripts so the content engine
  can run without you in the booth every time.

Both come from the SAME Nix. One voice, owned by you, used two ways.
