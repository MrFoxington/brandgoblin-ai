# YouTube From Scratch (2026)
### The brutally honest guide for a complete beginner starting a YouTube channel from zero.
> Based on fresh 2025-2026 research. Every major claim is sourced, and the full list of sources with links is at the bottom of this guide.

---

## WHAT THIS GUIDE IS AND HOW TO USE IT

This is a plain-language playbook for starting a YouTube channel from absolutely nothing, whether you run a home bakery, coach people on money, build apps, or want an audience around any other topic. It is built from current 2025-2026 research on how YouTube distributes videos, what realistic first-month numbers look like, and where beginners waste their hours. It assumes zero YouTube experience.

How to use it: read sections 1 and 2 once, slowly. Then treat sections 3 through 11 as checklists you come back to. If you only have ten minutes today, read section 2 (the brutal truths) and section 5 (the short-video pipeline), because those two cover 90 percent of what you will actually do in month one. Examples rotate between three sample niches, a home bakery, a personal-finance coach, and an indie app developer, so you can map everything onto your own topic.

Core premise, stated up front: in month one, YouTube's short-video feed (Shorts) is your only game, and if you also post short videos on TikTok or Instagram Reels, the exact same videos serve all three platforms for a few extra minutes per post. YouTube's superpower arrives later: it is the one major platform where content keeps working for months and where people actively SEARCH for exactly what you offer. Month one plants that garden. Months 2 and beyond harvest it. This guide is honest about that split everywhere.

## 1. HOW YOUTUBE ACTUALLY WORKS, IN PLAIN ENGLISH

YouTube is really two different machines wearing one trench coat. You need to understand both, because they reward different things.

### Machine 1: The Shorts feed (your month-one machine)

**What a Short is.** A Short is a vertical video up to 3 minutes long that plays in a swipe-up feed, exactly like TikTok's For You Page. The 3-minute limit is current and confirmed: YouTube raised it from 60 seconds to 180 seconds on October 15, 2024. One important side effect landed December 8, 2025: ANY video you upload that is vertical (or square) and 3 minutes or under is automatically classified as a Short. You cannot opt out. Remember this later when we talk about long-form: a long-form video must be horizontal, or YouTube will file it in the Shorts feed whether you like it or not. ([YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/15424877?hl=en), [Descript](https://www.descript.com/blog/article/how-long-can-youtube-shorts-be))

**How a new Short gets judged.** Every Short gets shown to a small test pool first, commonly cited as 50 to 500 viewers, and if it holds their attention it escalates to bigger pools. In 2026 that test pool is roughly 70 percent people who have never seen your channel, so you do not need subscribers for a Short to travel. Why this matters: your channel can be brand new and still compete, video by video. ([vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-algorithm/), [Miraflow](https://miraflow.ai/blog/how-youtube-algorithm-decides-who-sees-your-shorts-2026))

**What the Shorts algorithm grades on.** Two numbers dominate everything else:
1. **Viewed vs swiped away.** The percentage of people who actually watched your Short instead of instantly swiping past it. This metric exists by name in your YouTube analytics. Above 70 percent is strong, under 60 percent is weak. ([Retensis](https://retensis.com/blog/youtube-shorts-analytics-metrics-explained))
2. **Average view duration as a percentage.** How much of the video the average person watched. Practitioner benchmarks for getting pushed wider: roughly 65 percent retention on Shorts under 30 seconds, roughly 50 percent on 30 to 60 second Shorts. 50 to 64 percent is solid, below 35 percent means the hook or pacing failed. ([Socialync](https://www.socialync.io/blog/youtube-shorts-algorithm-2026), [Retensis](https://retensis.com/blog/youtube-shorts-analytics-metrics-explained))

After those two: shares (the strongest single engagement signal), replays, comments, and likes, in roughly that order. If you have built short-video instincts on TikTok, they apply directly here. ([Retensis](https://retensis.com/blog/youtube-shorts-analytics-metrics-explained))

**One accounting quirk worth knowing.** On March 31, 2025, YouTube changed what counts as a "view" on Shorts: now every play counts instantly, even a half-second swipe-past. The old, stricter count still exists under the name **engaged views**, and that is the number that matters for monetization and for judging yourself honestly. Translation: your public view counts will look flattering; check engaged views in analytics for the truth. ([TubeBuddy](https://www.tubebuddy.com/blog/youtube-shorts-view-count-update-what-creators-need-to-know-about-the-new-metrics/), [Gyre](https://gyre.pro/blog/youtube-shorts-view-count-update-impact-strategy-what-to-do-next))

### Machine 2: Long-form (the month 2+ machine)

Long-form is classic YouTube: horizontal videos people click on from their home page, search results, or the sidebar. Ranking here runs on two different numbers:
- **CTR, click-through rate:** of the people shown your video's thumbnail, what percentage clicked it. Driven almost entirely by your thumbnail (the cover image) and title. Healthy range is 4 to 10 percent, and small channels often sit at 6 to 10 percent because early viewers are mostly subscribers. ([Humble and Brag CTR benchmarks](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/youtube-ctr-benchmarks))
- **Retention / average view duration:** how long people actually watch after clicking. Above 50 percent of the video watched is healthy for most content; for videos under 5 minutes aim for 60 to 70 percent. ([Humble and Brag retention benchmarks](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/youtube-audience-retention-benchmarks))

Long-form traffic comes from three doors: **search** (people typing questions into YouTube), **browse** (the home page feed), and **suggested** (the sidebar next to other videos). For a small channel, search is the honest door: browse and suggested favor channels with history, but search ranks videos by how well they answer the query, which is winnable from day one if you target searches big channels ignore. A home baker can rank for "how to price custom cakes." A finance coach can rank for "budget for variable income." An app developer can rank for the exact problem their app solves. This is the long-form plan, and it lives in section 6. ([Backlinko](https://backlinko.com/how-to-rank-youtube-videos), [ProDigitalWeb](https://www.prodigitalweb.com/youtube-seo-for-beginners/))

### The differences from TikTok that actually change your behavior

If you also use TikTok, most of your instincts transfer as-is. Here is precisely what is DIFFERENT on YouTube (and if you skipped TikTok entirely, these still stand alone as rules):

1. **Links do not work in Shorts. At all.** Since August 31, 2023, URLs in Shorts descriptions and Shorts comments are NOT clickable. On TikTok a pinned-comment-plus-bio-link play works; on YouTube Shorts the only clickable links live on your channel page (the banner link and the About section). This single fact reshapes your whole conversion play, covered in section 8. ([YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/13748639?hl=en), [TubeBuddy](https://www.tubebuddy.com/blog/youtube-shorts-link-changes/))
2. **Shorts have a real title, and it behaves like a search listing.** TikTok has a caption; YouTube Shorts have a title that shows up in YouTube search and even Google. Write Shorts titles like tiny search results ("How to price a custom cake in 60 seconds"), not like TikTok captions ("not me underpricing again"). Keywords in the title and description carry more weight than hashtags on YouTube. ([Hashtag Tools](https://hashtagtools.io/blog/youtube-shorts-hashtags-title-vs-description-2026), [PostEverywhere](https://posteverywhere.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-vs-tiktok))
3. **Shorts age better.** TikTok's push is mostly a 24 to 72 hour event. Shorts keep collecting views for weeks or months through search and recommendations. Practical effect: never delete a "flopped" Short, and judge Shorts on a 7 day window, not 48 hours. ([Miraflow](https://miraflow.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-vs-tiktok-2026), [PostEverywhere](https://posteverywhere.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-vs-tiktok))
4. **New channels ramp slower.** TikTok gives total unknowns a fair viral shot on video one. On YouTube, early distribution leans on channel history and topic signals it has not learned yet, so growth is more gradual but more durable. Expect quieter first weeks than TikTok, with less lottery upside and less flop downside. ([Miraflow](https://miraflow.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-vs-tiktok-2026), [FluxNote](https://fluxnote.io/guides/youtube-shorts-vs-tiktok-algorithm-2026))
5. **No comment-reply-video button.** TikTok's "reply with video" feature (where a viewer's comment appears as a sticker on your response video) has no YouTube equivalent. The workaround: screenshot the YouTube comment, drop the screenshot into the video in your editor, and it works the same. In practice, YouTube comments become material for videos you make once and post everywhere.
6. **Trending sounds barely matter on Shorts.** TikTok's sound culture is a ranking lever. On Shorts, voiceover-led videos travel fine with no trending audio, and licensed music in Shorts over 60 seconds gets restricted anyway. Your voice is the audio strategy. One less thing to do per upload. ([YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/15424877?hl=en))
7. **Subscribers mean slightly more here.** Still not the main ranking input, but YouTube's test pool includes some subscribers, subscribers feed the long-form machine later, and YouTube counts "did this viewer subscribe after watching" as a satisfaction signal. On TikTok the standard advice is to ignore follower count. On YouTube, treat subscribers as a small but real asset you are quietly banking for month 2. ([vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-shorts-algorithm/))

One-line summary: same feed game as TikTok, but the links are locked, the titles are searchable, the videos live longer, and the channel is an asset that compounds instead of resetting with every post.

## 2. THE BRUTAL TRUTHS, UP FRONT

1. **Month one on YouTube will be quiet.** For a business channel under 5,000 subscribers, 100 to 300 views per Short in the first 48 hours is considered HEALTHY. Many of your first Shorts will do less. A realistic month across 20 to 25 Shorts: a few thousand to a few tens of thousands of total views, heavily lumpy, with most Shorts under 500 views and hopefully one or two that break out. ([Humble and Brag](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/new-youtube-channel-average-views), [Humble and Brag Shorts benchmarks](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/youtube-shorts-benchmarks))
2. **You cannot put a clickable link anywhere near a Short.** Not the description, not a pinned comment. That has been true platform-wide since August 2023 and no channel size unlocks it. Your clickable links live only on your channel page. Every YouTube viewer who becomes a customer will either type your URL from memory, visit your channel page first, or Google you. Each step leaks people. This is the single biggest reason Shorts convert worse than TikTok in month one, and it is a platform rule, not a skill problem. Design around it (section 8) instead of fighting it. ([YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/13748639?hl=en))
3. **Monetization is irrelevant to a beginner's goal, and far away anyway.** The YouTube Partner Program (the thing that lets channels earn ad money) currently needs 500 subscribers plus 3,000 watch hours or 3 million Shorts views in 90 days just for the junior tier, and 1,000 subscribers plus 4,000 watch hours or 10 million Shorts views for full ad revenue. If you are using YouTube to promote a business, you are not chasing any of that: you monetize when viewers become customers. This is mentioned only so you never waste an hour thinking about it. ([YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/72851?hl=en&co=GENIE.Platform%3DAndroid), [vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-partner-program-guide/))
4. **"Shorts don't convert" is partly true and worth respecting.** The pessimist case has real numbers behind it: one widely shared analysis found under 0.2 percent of Shorts viewers ever click through to a channel's long-form content, and Shorts audiences skew toward casual scrollers. The optimist case also has numbers: channels posting both Shorts and long-form reportedly grow subscribers around 3x faster than single-format channels, and business marketers overwhelmingly report positive return from short-form video. The honest synthesis: Shorts are a cheap awareness engine and a slow subscriber drip, not a sales faucet. Expect low single-digit direct results from Shorts alone in month one, and treat anything more as a bonus. ([Marketing Agent Blog](https://marketingagent.blog/2026/02/15/how-to-balance-youtube-shorts-and-long-form-content-for-maximum-roi-in-2026-optimizing-both-formats/), [AIR Media-Tech](https://air.io/en/youtube-hacks/should-you-chase-shorts-views-or-double-down-on-long-form-for-channel-growth), [Content Beta](https://www.contentbeta.com/blog/youtube-shorts-for-b2b-saas-specs-and-strategy/))
5. **Reposting with a TikTok watermark genuinely buries you.** This is not folklore on YouTube. YouTube runs watermark detection, and one documented test showed watermarked TikTok reposts averaging 450 views while the same content uploaded as a clean original file averaged 12,000. The fix is free: always keep the clean exported file from your editing app and upload that. It is the one repurposing sin YouTube truly punishes. ([Joyspace](https://joyspace.ai/stop-reposting-tiktoks-watermark-detection), [Socialync](https://www.socialync.io/blog/avoid-content-duplication-penalties-cross-posting-2026))
6. **Subscriber count is vanity in month one. Two numbers are not.** Viewed vs swiped away, and average view duration percentage. Those decide distribution. Subscribers follow them. The one exception: 500 subscribers eventually unlocks the junior monetization tier and, more usefully, signals YouTube that the channel is real. Bank subs, do not chase them.
7. **Most business channels flop for the same reason as on every platform: they post ads.** Polished, logo-first, salesy content dies in the feed everywhere. Raw, entertaining, product-as-payoff content wins. The channels that flop on YouTube add a second failure mode all their own: uploading horizontal 10-minute walkthroughs with zero search targeting that nobody was ever going to find. Avoid both.

## 3. CHANNEL SETUP, STEP BY STEP

Jargon for this section. Your **handle** is your @name. The **banner** is the wide image across the top of your channel page. The **About section** is your channel's description page, and it is one of the only places YouTube gives you clickable links. **Verification** here means confirming a phone number, which unlocks custom thumbnails and other features; it is not the gray checkmark badge.

### Step 1: Create the channel (10 minutes)
1. Go to youtube.com signed in with a Google account, click your avatar, Create a channel.
2. **Channel name:** your business or personal brand name. **Handle:** matching your TikTok and Instagram exactly if you use them. One name across all platforms, because when a video says "search [your name]," that search has to work everywhere.
3. Turn on two-step verification on the Google account. Losing the account later hurts.

### Step 2: Verify the phone number immediately (5 minutes)
Go to youtube.com/verify and confirm your phone. This unlocks custom thumbnails (cover images you design instead of a random frame), videos over 15 minutes, and eventually the "related video" link feature on Shorts. Do it on day one so it is never a blocker. ([Castos](https://castos.com/how-to-start-a-youtube-channel/))

### Step 3: Branding (30 minutes, reusing assets from your other platforms if you have them)
- **Profile photo:** the same close-cropped image you use everywhere else. It must read at 36 pixels, the size it renders on mobile. ([Subscribr](https://subscribr.ai/p/youtube-channel-setup-checklist))
- **Banner:** 2560 x 1440 pixels, but keep all text and imagery inside the center 1546 x 423 "safe zone" or TVs and phones will crop it. Content: your best visual, your one-line promise (the baker: "Custom cakes worth the calories." The coach: "Debt-free plans for normal incomes."), and your URL in plain text. Canva's free tier can make this in 20 minutes.
- **Channel description (the About section):** write it with search keywords, because channel descriptions are indexed. Finance coach example: "I help families on normal incomes make budgets that survive real life: debt payoff plans, variable income budgeting, and money habits that stick. Free starter template at [yoursite.com]." Name your topics literally; this is a search document, not poetry.
- **Links:** in channel customization, add your links. The FIRST one becomes the clickable link on your banner, so it must be your main URL, with a tracking tag like `?ref=yt` so your website statistics can tell you which visitors came from YouTube. Add your other social profiles after it. Sources disagree on the current maximum shown (5 vs 14); it does not matter, the first link is the one doing the work. ([Subscribr](https://subscribr.ai/p/youtube-channel-setup-checklist), [Accio](https://www.accio.com/blog/how-to-add-links-to-your-youtube-channel-a-guide))

### Step 4: Upload defaults (10 minutes, saves time forever)
In YouTube Studio, Settings, Upload defaults, set once:
- Default description: two lines about what you do, then your URL with a note like "(link is clickable on our channel page)" then 3 hashtags: #Shorts plus two describing your niche. The URL is not clickable in Shorts but people do read and retype it, and the same default serves future long-form where links ARE clickable.
- Default visibility: Private (so you consciously publish each one).
- Category: pick the one that fits your niche (Howto & Style covers most business content) and stay consistent so YouTube learns the channel's topic faster.

### Step 5: Housekeeping that quietly matters
- Create one playlist now for your signature series (the baker: "Your Cake Requests, Baked." The developer: "Features You Asked For."). Playlists are minor SEO and give binge-watchers a rail.
- Skip the channel trailer until you have a long-form video worth featuring (month 2).
- No need for a "Brand Account" or any paid anything. A standard channel does everything you need.

Total setup: about one hour, once.

## 4. THE MONTH-1 STRATEGY VERDICT: SHORTS-ONLY, AND HERE IS THE MATH

**Verdict: month one is Shorts-only, ideally 100 percent reused from short videos you are already making for TikTok and Reels. Zero long-form until month 2 at the earliest.**

The reasoning, with numbers:

1. **The hour cost of long-form is brutal for a beginner.** Editing alone for a single 10-minute video runs 5+ hours for a beginner, and tutorial-style content with screen recordings and graphics commonly takes 6 to 12 hours total per video once you count scripting, recording, editing, thumbnail, and title work. That is one to two full working days. ([Gling](https://www.gling.ai/blog/how-long-does-it-really-take-to-edit-a-youtube-video), [This Girl Edits](https://thisgirledits.com/how-long-does-it-take-to-edit-a-youtube-video/))
2. **Those same hours in month one are worth more elsewhere.** Your first month should go to learning the short-video craft and getting reps in, which produces results in week-one timeframes. A long-form search video pays off over months (search rankings build slowly), which is exactly wrong for your first 30 days.
3. **Shorts reuse costs almost nothing.** If you make short videos for any platform, the marginal cost of also posting them to YouTube is a searchable title and a description tweak, 3 to 5 minutes per video. Twenty-five posts at 5 minutes is roughly 2 hours for the entire month. Any result above zero makes that trade worth it. If YouTube is your ONLY platform, the same logic holds in reverse: build your short-video system here first (the TikTok-style production habits in section 5 apply), and add long-form later.
4. **The hybrid-growth evidence is real but not urgent.** Yes, channels mixing Shorts and long-form reportedly grow around 3x faster, and the standard 2026 advice is 3 to 5 Shorts a week plus 1 to 2 long-form a month. That is your month 2+ shape, once the first 30 days have produced real data on which topics hit. Section 6 has the on-ramp ready. ([Miraflow](https://miraflow.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-vs-long-form-which-grows-channel-faster-2026), [Aidelly](https://www.aidelly.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-vs-long-videos-the-complete-growth-strategy-for-beginners-in-2026))

What you give up by waiting: essentially nothing in the 30-day window. What you gain: a calibrated channel, a few hundred subscribers-in-progress, and real data on which of your topics resonate before you spend 8 hours on one video.

## 5. THE SHORTS PIPELINE: FINISHED SHORT VIDEO TO POSTED SHORT

This is the whole month-one workflow. It assumes you make short vertical videos somewhere (in an editor like CapCut, whether your primary home is TikTok, Reels, or YouTube itself). Time per video: 5 to 10 minutes, dropping to 3 to 5 once it is habit.

**Step 1: Use the clean file, always.** The video comes from your editor's export to your camera roll, never downloaded from TikTok or any other platform. Platform downloads carry watermarks, and watermarked Shorts get buried (the documented 450 vs 12,000 view case). If you ever lose a clean file, re-export from the editing project rather than using a watermark remover; removers leave traces YouTube's detection can still flag. ([Joyspace](https://joyspace.ai/stop-reposting-tiktoks-watermark-detection))

**Step 2: Check the sound.** If the TikTok version used a trending sound added inside TikTok, that sound does not exist in your clean file, which is fine: your voiceover carries the video. Do not bother hunting a matching YouTube sound. If a video's whole joke depended on a specific trending sound, skip posting that one to Shorts.

**Step 3: Upload natively in the YouTube app.** Tap the plus button, select the video, and confirm it is being treated as a Short (vertical, under 3 minutes, it will be automatically). Cross-posting between platforms carries no penalty; the platforms cannot see each other's uploads. Only the watermark matters. ([Socialync](https://www.socialync.io/blog/avoid-content-duplication-penalties-cross-posting-2026))

**Step 4: Write a TITLE, not a caption. This is the big behavioral difference.** A TikTok caption is conversational bait. A Shorts title is a search listing. Formula: what happens plus a keyword someone might search. Under 70 characters, keywords first.
- TikTok caption: "The man who hates cake has been defeated. What should I bake next?"
- Same video's Shorts title: "Custom cake for someone who hates cake (full decorating process)"
- Finance coach example: "Budgeting a variable income in 3 steps"
- App developer example: "I built the feature you all asked for (live demo)"

**Step 5: Description and hashtags.** Your upload default already handles most of it. Add one video-specific line up top restating the title with different words. Keep hashtags to 3 to 5 in the description, including #Shorts. More than 15 total and YouTube ignores all of them. Hashtags are a minor assist here, same as TikTok in 2026; the title and description keywords do the real work. ([Hashtag Tools](https://hashtagtools.io/blog/youtube-shorts-hashtags-title-vs-description-2026), [Miraflow](https://miraflow.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-hashtags-2026-do-they-still-matter))

**Step 6: Pin a comment.** Something like: "Orders and the free guide are at [yoursite.com]. Not clickable here, YouTube's rule, but the link on our channel page is." Honest, human, and it preempts the "where's the link" comment. The URL is not clickable but it is readable, and naming the channel-page link teaches viewers where the real door is.

**Step 7: Timing, the low-stakes version.** Shorts skew toward afternoon and evening viewing (rough consensus: 2 to 4 PM and 8 to 11 PM), but timing is a weak variable on YouTube for the same reason as TikTok: the feed is not chronological, and Shorts collect views for weeks. Post whenever you post everywhere else and stop thinking about it. Consistency beats clock-watching. ([Hopper HQ](https://www.hopperhq.com/blog/best-time-to-post-youtube-shorts/), [PostEverywhere](https://posteverywhere.ai/blog/best-time-to-schedule-youtube-shorts))

**Step 8 (weekly, 10 minutes): the Sunday sweep.** Once a week, open YouTube Studio: reply to every comment, add new videos to your playlist, and log the numbers from section 7. YouTube comments requesting things ("can you do a graduation cake," "what about budgeting as a student") go into the same idea pile as comments from every other platform and become next week's videos.

**What NOT to change per video:** no new edits, no re-cutting for YouTube, no platform-specific versions, no end screens (Shorts do not have them), no custom thumbnails for Shorts (the feed ignores them). The video is done. YouTube gets the copy.

**Batching:** if daily posting ever slips, YouTube Studio on desktop lets you schedule Shorts in advance. Uploading a week of Shorts on Sunday and scheduling one per day is a legitimate pattern and beats burst-posting then vanishing, which is the most damaging pattern on every platform. ([Meteorra](https://www.meteorra.ai/blog/how-often-should-you-post-on-tiktok-youtube-shorts-instagram-reels))

## 6. THE LONG-FORM ON-RAMP FOR MONTH 2+

When your first 30 days end, long-form becomes worth the hours, for one reason: **search intent.** People type their exact problem into YouTube at the exact moment they have it. A video ranking for that search is a salesperson that works every day for years. Budget: ONE long-form video per month, 6 to 10 hours all-in as a beginner, targeting searches, not the browse feed. Find topics with the free method: type your seed words into YouTube's search bar and write down what autocomplete suggests, because those suggestions are real searches ranked by frequency. Target the specific, lower-competition phrasings. ([SEO Sherpa](https://seosherpa.com/youtube-seo/), [Backlinko](https://backlinko.com/how-to-rank-youtube-videos))

Ground rules for all long-form: horizontal video (remember, vertical under 3 minutes auto-files as a Short), 6 to 10 minutes long, your voiceover over footage or screen recording (no face needed if you prefer), custom thumbnail, keyword in the first half of the title and the first two sentences of the description, clickable link with your `?ref=yt` tag in the description, end screen in the last 20 seconds. An **end screen** is the clickable panel YouTube lets you overlay on a video's final seconds, pointing to another video or your channel; the best-performing layout is one video element plus one subscribe button, and business channels see 5 to 8 percent of viewers click them. ([Humble and Brag](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/youtube-end-screens))

Three fully worked concepts, one per example niche, so you can see the shape and copy it:

### Concept 1 (home bakery): the pricing video (highest pain, highest trust)
- **Working title:** "How to Price Custom Cakes in 2026 (Real Numbers From My Home Bakery)"
- **Thumbnail concept:** split screen. Left: a beautiful cake with "$40?" in gray. Right: the same cake with "$240" in big bright type and your face mid-grimace. Three words of text max: "STOP UNDERCHARGING."
- **Outline:** (1) Cold open, 20 seconds: "This cake took 11 hours. I charged $45 for my first one. Here's the formula I wish I'd had." (2) The real cost breakdown people never do: ingredients, hours, overhead, one minute each. (3) The formula, worked live on screen with a real order. (4) The three pricing mistakes that kill home bakeries, one minute. (5) The awkward part: how to say the price out loud without apologizing, one minute. (6) Recap card plus end screen to your next video.

### Concept 2 (finance coach): the how-to video (biggest search volume in the niche)
- **Working title:** "How to Make a Budget You'll Actually Keep (5 Steps, No Apps Required)"
- **Thumbnail concept:** a crossed-out complicated spreadsheet on the left, a single index card with five lines on the right, big arrow between them. Text: "THIS ACTUALLY WORKS."
- **Outline:** (1) Cold open: "Every budget you've quit failed for the same reason, and it wasn't discipline," 20 seconds. (2) The design flaw in most budgets: they plan an ideal month, one minute. (3) Steps one through five, one minute each, all doable by hand today, with a real (anonymized) example threading through all five. (4) What to do the first time it breaks, because it will, one minute. (5) "The free template is in the description," recap, end screen.

### Concept 3 (indie app developer): the honest demo video (lower search volume, highest conversion)
- **Working title:** "I Built an App That [Solves the Exact Problem] (Full Live Demo)"
- **Thumbnail concept:** a stopwatch or a before/after of the problem next to the app's result, your face or the app icon reacting. Text: three words naming the outcome.
- **Outline:** (1) The promise and the problem, 15 seconds. (2) The unedited real-time demo with your live reactions, three to four minutes; the authenticity of unedited waiting is the point. (3) Walking through what it made or did, two minutes. (4) What the free version includes versus paid, 30 seconds, plain and honest, including what it is NOT good at. (5) "The link is in the description, tell me what to build next in the comments." End screen to your best other video.

Make one per month, months 2 through 4, each targeting a search you verified in autocomplete. Each is evergreen, each links to the others, and every Short you post can point at them with the **related video** feature: a link YouTube shows under a Short that leads viewers to one of your own long-form videos. That is the one legitimate clickable pathway out of the Shorts feed, it requires the channel verification you did in section 3, and it is set per-Short from YouTube Studio on desktop. ([vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-related-links-connect-shorts-long-videos/), [TubeBuddy](https://www.tubebuddy.com/blog/youtube-shorts-related-video-manager-strategy/))

## 7. READING THE NUMBERS

Where to look: YouTube Studio app or studio.youtube.com, Analytics, then tap any Short for its own stats. Give a Short 7 days before judging (longer memory than TikTok's 48 hours).

### Shorts: the four numbers that matter

| Metric | Plain-English meaning | Good | Bad sign |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Viewed vs swiped away** | % who watched instead of instantly swiping past | 70%+ | under 60%: first frame or first second failed |
| **Average % viewed** | how much of the Short the average viewer watched | 65%+ on sub-30s Shorts, 50%+ on longer ones | under 35%: hook or pacing problem, fix before posting more of that format |
| **Engaged views** | the honest view count (watched more than a moment) | trending up week over week | huge gap between raw views and engaged views: swipe-bait numbers, not real reach |
| **Subscribers gained per Short** | new subs attributed to that video | any nonzero number, banked for month 2 | consistently zero on 1,000+ view Shorts: content entertains but the channel gives no reason to stay |

Benchmarks from [Retensis](https://retensis.com/blog/youtube-shorts-analytics-metrics-explained), [Humble and Brag](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/youtube-shorts-benchmarks), and [Shortimize](https://www.shortimize.com/blog/youtube-shorts-retention-rate).

### Decision rules (run in the weekly sweep)

- **If you post the same videos on multiple platforms, do not optimize Shorts separately.** Same videos, same formats, so your primary platform's kill/keep/double-down decisions already govern production. YouTube analytics get a vote only when a video behaves differently across platforms: if something flops on TikTok but runs on Shorts, note the topic, because that is a YouTube-audience signal and a future long-form candidate.
- **Watch for search-driven Shorts.** In a Short's analytics, traffic sources showing meaningful "YouTube search" traffic means the title keywords are working. Whatever that Short is about is your strongest long-form topic evidence.
- **The gap check:** if viewed-vs-swiped is fine but average % viewed is weak, the middle sags. If viewed-vs-swiped is weak, the first frame fails. Same diagnosis logic as a TikTok retention graph.

### Long-form benchmarks (for month 2+, parked here so they are ready)

- **CTR:** 4 to 10 percent healthy; small channels often 6 to 10 because early impressions go to subscribers. Under 3 percent from search traffic: fix the thumbnail or title. ([Humble and Brag](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/youtube-ctr-benchmarks))
- **Retention:** 50 percent average view duration is healthy for most lengths; 60 to 70 percent for videos under 5 minutes. Check the retention graph for the cliff, fix that beat next video. ([Humble and Brag](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/youtube-audience-retention-benchmarks))
- **End screen click rate:** 3 to 8 percent normal, 5 to 8 is a good business-channel target. ([Humble and Brag](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/youtube-end-screens))
- **Return viewers above 10 percent** means an actual audience is forming, not just passersby. ([Humble and Brag](https://humbleandbrag.com/blog/new-youtube-channel-average-views))

## 8. TURNING VIEWS INTO RESULTS, GIVEN THE LINK LOCKDOWN

The funnel on YouTube Shorts has one extra leak that TikTok does not: no clickable link anywhere near the video. The honest funnel: Short, then either (a) viewer remembers and types your URL, (b) viewer taps your channel, then the banner link or About link, or (c) viewer Googles your name. Design for all three:

1. **The spoken CTA is your whole conversion engine.** "It's on my site, just search [your name]" spoken plainly at the payoff moment. Saying your name matters double on YouTube because search is native behavior here; someone typing it into YouTube or Google finds you instantly, which is also why your name must be easy to spell. If any part of your offer is free, say "free" every time; it is the strongest single word in conversion.
2. **Readable text URL everywhere.** On-screen text overlay at the payoff, in the description, in the pinned comment. Not clickable, still copied and retyped by the motivated few. The motivated few are exactly who becomes customers.
3. **The channel page is the landing page.** Banner link first (with the `?ref=yt` tag), About section selling in two lines, playlist of your best work up top. Every profile tap should meet a page that makes the next step obvious.
4. **Related video links (month 2+):** once long-form exists, set every Short's related-video link to the matching long-form video. The long-form description holds the clickable link and the end screen reinforces it. This is the designed pathway: Short catches attention, long-form earns trust, description link converts. ([vidIQ](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/youtube-related-links-connect-shorts-long-videos/))
5. **Realistic conversion math, so nothing here disappoints you.** Standard short-video math says roughly 0.5 to 1 percent of viewers click through and 2 to 5 percent of visitors act. On Shorts, cut the click-through step in half again because of the link friction: 20,000 Shorts views in month one might mean 50 to 150 site visits and 2 to 7 real results. Long-form flips this completely: fewer views, but viewers chose to click a search result, links are clickable, and description-link clickers from a how-to video are warm. A single ranking long-form video can quietly out-convert a hundred Shorts. That asymmetry is the entire argument for section 6.
6. **Track it honestly:** the `?ref=yt` tag on the channel link, watched in your site analytics alongside your other platforms' tags. If YouTube's line stays at zero for two weeks while another platform's moves, nothing is broken; this platform just has a longer fuse. Keep the 5 minutes a day and reassess at day 30 with real data.

## 9. MISTAKES THAT KILL CHANNELS (THE BLUNT LIST)

The universal short-video sins apply here too (making ads, engagement bait, quitting during the quiet weeks, buying subscribers, corporate comment replies). These are the YouTube-specific additions:

1. **Uploading a watermarked file.** The one repurposing crime with documented punishment (450 vs 12,000 views). Clean editor exports only, forever.
2. **Treating Shorts titles like TikTok captions.** A vibes-only caption throws away YouTube's search discovery, which is the platform's one structural gift to small channels.
3. **Putting hashtag soup in the title.** Hashtags go in the description, 3 to 5 of them; more than 15 anywhere and YouTube ignores them all.
4. **Deleting or privating "flopped" Shorts.** Shorts resurface via search weeks later. A flop costs nothing and might wake up.
5. **Uploading a vertical "long-form" video.** Vertical and under 3 minutes auto-classifies as a Short since December 2025. Long-form means horizontal, no exceptions.
6. **Chasing monetization thresholds.** 10 million Shorts views in 90 days is the full-monetization Shorts bar. If you have a business, customers are your business model, not ad pennies.
7. **Begging for subscribers in every Short.** "Subscribe for more" as a bare demand is the same ad-smell as engagement bait anywhere. Earn subs with the content; ask, at most, once per video softly, or not at all.
8. **Judging YouTube by TikTok's clock.** Quieter first 48 hours is the platform's normal shape, not failure. Seven-day judgments, 30-day verdicts.
9. **Building YouTube-only short content in month one.** The whole economics of this channel rests on marginal cost near zero. If Shorts demand their own production pipeline this month, the plan is broken. One shoot, every platform.

## 10. THE 30-DAY YOUTUBE CALENDAR

This adds almost no new production if you are already making short videos. Every item is minutes.

**Days 1 to 3 (setup window):**
- Create the channel, verify the phone, complete branding, links, upload defaults, the one playlist. One hour total, once.
- Spend 10 minutes a day in the YouTube app watching Shorts in your niche (search your topic's main words) so the app learns you and you learn the Shorts flavor of your niche: slightly more explanatory, slightly less trend-driven than TikTok. ([PostEverywhere](https://posteverywhere.ai/blog/youtube-shorts-vs-tiktok))

**Day 4 (first videos ship):** your first short videos go to Shorts via the section 5 pipeline. Searchable titles, pinned comments, done. 10 extra minutes.

**Days 5 to 14:** every short video you make gets its Shorts twin the same day, 3 to 5 minutes each. Weekly sweep on days 7 and 14: reply to YouTube comments, playlist upkeep, log the four numbers from section 7 plus any `?ref=yt` results.

**Days 15 to 21:** the pipeline continues on autopilot. If anything you do this month spikes attention elsewhere (a viral post, a local news mention, a launch), some of that traffic will search your name on YouTube; the channel existing with 15+ Shorts is the point.

**Days 22 to 30 (double down week):** whatever your best-performing format is, make 3 to 5 variations of it; they flow to Shorts automatically. Then check one YouTube-specific thing: did any Short meaningfully outperform its TikTok twin, or pull search traffic? Note the topic; that is your first long-form video's subject, possibly overriding section 6's suggested order.
- Day 30: the honest recap. Spend 15 minutes on the YouTube verdict: total Shorts views, engaged views, subscribers, results at `?ref=yt`, and whether any topic earned the month-2 long-form hours.

**The month's honest scoreboard for YouTube alone:** 20 to 25 Shorts posted (all reused from your main production), 5,000 to 40,000 total views (wide because variance rules), 30 to 150 subscribers, low single-digit direct results, and one validated long-form topic. Total incremental time cost: roughly 3 to 4 hours across the entire month. That trade is the whole point.

## 11. WHAT TO AUTOMATE OR AI-ASSIST, HONESTLY

Worth automating or delegating to AI:
- **Shorts titles and descriptions.** Feed your caption and the video's topic to an AI assistant, ask for a sub-70-character searchable title plus a two-line keyworded description. This is the main YouTube-specific writing task and it is perfectly delegable; you approve, you post.
- **Scheduling.** YouTube Studio's built-in desktop scheduler for the week's Shorts on batch day. Free, native, zero risk.
- **Keyword scouting for month 2.** AI can expand your autocomplete findings into a candidate list of long-form search targets. Validation stays manual: you type them into YouTube yourself and eyeball what currently ranks.
- **Thumbnail drafts (month 2+).** Canva plus AI-suggested layouts for A and B versions. Keep the final call human; the thumbnail is the highest-leverage 30 minutes of any long-form video.

Never automate:
- **Your voiceover.** YouTube's 2026 systems actively suppress low-quality AI content, and your real voice is the moat. ([BibiGPT](https://bibigpt.co/en/blog/posts/youtube-2026-ai-roadmap-1m-channels-bibigpt-en))
- **Comment replies.** Small comment volume in month one makes this easy: reply as yourself, humanly, in the weekly sweep.
- **Fully AI-generated Shorts pipelines.** The faceless-automation channels are precisely what the platform is now demoting. Your human-made content is the opposite of that and benefits from the contrast.

Rule of thumb: automate the paperwork around the content, never the human inside it.
