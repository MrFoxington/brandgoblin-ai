## 1. HOW X ACTUALLY WORKS, IN PLAIN ENGLISH

**What X is, mechanically.** X (the app formerly called Twitter) is a feed of short text posts, images, and videos. Unlike TikTok, text is the native language here. The crowd skews toward tech people, founders, marketers, news junkies, and people who like arguing. That crowd matters for you: it is where indie founders (solo people building software businesses, like you) hang out and talk shop.

**The two feeds.** When you open X you see two tabs. "Following" shows posts only from accounts you follow, newest first. "For You" is the main feed, built by a computer program that picks posts it thinks you will engage with, including posts from total strangers. Most people live in For You. That is how a nobody can get seen: the machine can put your post in front of strangers if it grades well. ([SocialBee](https://socialbee.com/blog/twitter-algorithm/), [Buffer](https://buffer.com/resources/twitter-timeline-algorithm/))

**"The algorithm" is just the grading system.** Parts of X's ranking code were made public, so we know roughly how actions are weighted when the machine decides whether to spread your post further. The commonly cited scoring, per action from a viewer: a repost (someone sharing your post to their followers) is worth roughly 20 points, a reply is worth roughly 13.5, a click into your profile about 12, a click on a link about 11, a bookmark (someone saving your post) about 10, and a like about 1. Read that again: one thoughtful reply is worth more than a dozen likes. Posts that start conversations win. Posts that collect quiet likes go nowhere. ([PostEverywhere](https://posteverywhere.ai/blog/how-the-x-twitter-algorithm-works), [AdLibrary](https://adlibrary.com/guides/x-twitter-algorithm-explained))

**The first hour decides everything.** X watches how a post performs in roughly its first 30 to 60 minutes. Strong early engagement means wider distribution. Weak early engagement means the post is done. For an account under 1,000 followers the bar is brutally low, but so is your supply of early viewers, which is why sections 5 and 6 are built around borrowing other people's audiences instead of shouting into your own empty room. ([FansGurus](https://fansgurus.com/blog/why-tweets-get-no-views-2026), [Teract](https://www.teract.ai/resources/twitter-algorithm-2026))

**Links are punished. This is the single most important fact for a product account.** X wants people to stay on X, so it buries posts that send people away. The evidence is not subtle:
- Posts with an external link in the main text get roughly 30 to 50 percent less initial reach than the same post without a link, and the penalty was tightened in early 2026. ([Tweet Archivist](https://www.tweetarchivist.com/how-twitter-algorithm-works-2025), [OpenTweet](https://opentweet.io/blog/how-twitter-x-algorithm-works-2026))
- Buffer analyzed 18.8 million posts from 71,000 accounts: since March 2026, link posts from non-paying accounts see close to zero median engagement. Effectively invisible. Paying accounts posting the same links still take a hit but remain viable. ([Buffer](https://buffer.com/resources/links-on-x/))
- The standard workarounds still work: put the link in the first reply under your post instead of in the post itself, or tell people the link is in your bio. Section 8 covers exactly where your links live. ([Medium](https://medium.com/@karim2k/i-hated-most-of-elons-changes-but-removing-link-penalties-he-finally-got-one-right-873aa18d5025))

**Other things the machine punishes:** being muted or blocked by enough people lowers your reach everywhere, not just with those users. Copy-paste replies, spammy link dropping, and engagement bait get accounts quietly suppressed (section 9). Since March 2026, reply quality is a direct ranking signal, and Premium users can downvote replies flagged as AI-generated or spammy, so lazy AI replies now actively hurt you. ([AdLibrary](https://adlibrary.com/guides/x-twitter-algorithm-explained), [SocialBee](https://socialbee.com/blog/twitter-algorithm/))

**A 2026 platform note: X Communities are gone.** Communities were X's version of topic groups, and older guides recommend them heavily for small accounts. X permanently shut them down on May 30, 2026. Ignore any advice that mentions them. The replacements (group chats and AI custom timelines) are not growth channels for you this month. ([Postory](https://postory.io/blog/grow-on-x-after-communities-shutdown), [OpenTweet](https://opentweet.io/blog/x-communities-shutting-down-what-creators-need-to-know))

### The Premium question, answered plainly

Premium is X's paid subscription. Three tiers in 2026: Basic at about $3 a month, Premium at about $8 a month, Premium+ at about $40 a month. ([X Help Center](https://help.x.com/en/using-x/x-premium), [TweetHunter](https://tweethunter.io/blog/twitter-blue-vs-x-premium))

What the data says: multiple independent studies, including Buffer's 18 million post analysis, find Premium accounts get roughly 6 to 10 times more impressions per post than free accounts, with paid replies also placed higher under big posts (which matters enormously for the reply strategy in section 6). And per the link data above, free accounts posting links are now essentially invisible. ([Buffer](https://buffer.com/resources/x-premium-review/), [Social Media Today](https://www.socialmediatoday.com/news/report-shows-paying-for-x-twitter-premkum-has-significant-reach-benefits/801881/), [Nerd Techy](https://nerdtechy.com/does-x-premium-actually-increase-your-reach))

**The verdict: pay the $8.** Yes, your X ad budget is $0, but Premium is not an ad, it is a tool subscription, and the acquisition plan already has a $50 tools buffer. In 2026, X is functionally a two-tier platform, and a product account that needs to occasionally share a link is exactly the kind of account the free tier punishes hardest. Eight dollars a month buys reply priority (your replies appear higher under big accounts, which is your entire growth strategy), the reach multiplier, longer posts, and an edit button. If the buffer is truly spoken for, Basic at $3 gets you reply priority and most of the value. Going fully free is possible but you would be playing the game on hard mode for the price of two coffees. This is the rare pay-to-play call where the honest answer is: pay.

## 2. THE BRUTAL TRUTHS, UP FRONT

Read these before you post anything.

1. **Posting into the void does not work, and X is the platform where this is most true.** On TikTok, every video gets a test audience of strangers even with zero followers. On X, your fresh account's posts will get roughly 20 to 100 views each, mostly from bots and nobody. There is no test-batch mercy here. A zero-follower account grows by replying under accounts that already have audiences, not by posting and praying. This is the whole strategic difference between your two channels. ([FansGurus](https://fansgurus.com/blog/why-tweets-get-no-views-2026), [IndieRadar](https://indieradar.app/blog/reply-guy-method-grow-x-twitter-zero-followers))

2. **Realistic month one, executed well:** 100 to 300 followers, most original posts under 500 views, a few posts in the 1,000 to 10,000 impression range when a reply or a scoreboard update lands, and 5 to 15 signups tagged ?ref=x. Accounts claiming "0 to 10k followers in 30 days" are lying, bought followers, or ran ads. One documented outlier got 560,000 impressions in 10 days from zero followers purely through fast, sharp replies to big accounts, so the ceiling is real, but it is a ceiling, not a plan. ([Graham Mann](https://grahammann.net/blog/how-to-grow-on-x-twitter-2026), [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/how-i-got-560k-impressions-on-x-in-10-days-with-zero-followers-and-zero-ad-spend-33c1e4d720), [Teract](https://www.teract.ai/resources/grow-twitter-following-2026))

3. **X is honestly a weaker signup channel than TikTok for BrandGoblin, and you should know why.** Your buyers are side hustlers, Etsy sellers, and dreamers with a business idea. That crowd scrolls TikTok. X's crowd is founders, developers, and marketers, people who might admire your journey and even sign up to poke at the tool, but the platform's median engagement rate has fallen to around 0.015 percent, overall engagement dropped about 9 percent in 2026, and multiple honest comparisons conclude early-stage founders get better return per hour from Reddit and short video. X earns its place in your plan for three things TikTok cannot do: the public challenge story, founder relationships (future collabs, launch-day support, feedback), and being visible in the room where indie makers talk. Value it for those, not raw signups. ([SociaVault](https://sociavault.com/blog/twitter-x-engagement-declining-2026-data), [Redship](https://redship.io/blog/reddit-vs-twitter-x-marketing), [OGTool](https://ogtool.com/blog/best-social-media-platform-startups-reddit-linkedin-twitter-roi-guide))

4. **The bot problem is real and you will feel it.** Estimates put bots at roughly 1 in 7 to 1 in 10 active accounts. Your replies will get AI-generated responses, your posts will attract crypto spam, and some of your early "followers" will be junk. X ran crackdowns in February and March 2026 (killed API-posted replies, banned spam automation), which helped, but plan on ignoring a lot of noise. Do not buy followers or use auto-reply tools, ever; the same crackdown machinery that catches bots catches accounts that behave like bots. ([Spider AF](https://spideraf.com/articles/twitter-bot-problem-how-bad-is-it-for-businesses), [Fireply](https://fireply.ai/blog/x-reply-bots-2026), [OpenTweet](https://opentweet.io/blog/twitter-automation-rules-2026))

5. **Engagement farming is a game you should refuse to play.** Engagement farming means posting deliberately provocative or bait content ("unpopular opinion: ..." rage bait, fake polls, "repost if you agree") purely to juice the metrics. The algorithm does reward outrage in the short term, and X even pays high-engagement Premium accounts a revenue share, which is why your feed is full of it. But it attracts the wrong audience, earns mutes and blocks (which cut your reach platform-wide), and builds nothing. Your version of "engagement content" is a genuine game: people reply with business ideas, Nix brands them. Real engagement, real product demo, no bait. ([Teract](https://www.teract.ai/resources/twitter-algorithm-2026), [OpenTweet](https://opentweet.io/blog/twitter-automation-rules-2026))

6. **Build-in-public still works, but only the honest, numeric kind.** Build-in-public means openly sharing your startup's real numbers, wins, and failures as content. The generic version ("just shipped a new feature! 🚀") is dead, saturated beyond parody. What still works in 2026: concrete challenges with public scoreboards, real revenue and signup numbers, and failure stories told straight. Some successful founders have actually gone quiet to avoid copycats, which tells you transparency is a real asset with a real cost. At your stage the copycat risk is near zero and the attention value is high, so transparency is your correct play. Your Day 6 challenge post from the master plan is exactly the format that survives. ([Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/lifestyle/is-this-the-end-of-build-in-public-heres-why-top-indie-hackers-are-suddenly-disappearing-IhSJQBnXNuNwSuNTuz4t), [Luka](https://luka.to/blog/product-hunt-dead-indie-hackers-first-users-2026), [VibrantSnap](https://www.vibrantsnap.com/blog/build-in-public-complete-guide-founders))

7. **The math of clicks, so your expectations are sane.** Organic posts convert impressions to link clicks at roughly 0.5 to 1.5 percent, and that is when a link is even present. Say you generate 50,000 total impressions this month (realistic with good replies): maybe 1,500 of those are on posts or replies where your link or bio is one step away, maybe 100 to 300 people visit your profile, maybe 30 to 100 click through, and at your landing page's 2 to 5 percent signup rate plus direct type-ins, you land at 5 to 15 signups. That is the honest funnel. One viral scoreboard post can double it. ([Webeyez](https://webeyez.com/insights/guides/average-click-through-rate-twitter), [WebFX](https://www.webfx.com/blog/social-media/x-twitter-marketing-benchmarks/))

## 3. ACCOUNT SETUP, STEP BY STEP

Jargon for this section: your "handle" is your @name. Your "bio" is the short text on your profile. A "pinned post" is one post you stick to the top of your profile so every visitor sees it first. A "header" is the wide banner image at the top of your profile.

1. **Create the account** with your email. Handle: `@brandgoblinai` if free, otherwise `@nixthegoblin` or `@foxbuildsnix`. Keep it short, sayable, and matching your TikTok name so people can find you across platforms. Turn on two-factor login immediately.
2. **Profile photo:** the canonical Nix face art, cropped close. Even though you post as the founder (section 4), Nix's face is the memorable visual asset, and profile photos on X render tiny. A green goblin face is instantly recognizable in a reply pile of human headshots and AI avatars. That is a genuine edge in the reply strategy.
3. **Header image:** a screenshot collage of one great brand kit BrandGoblin generated, with one line of text: "Type an idea. Get a brand. 2 minutes." The header is free billboard space most people waste.
4. **Bio (160 characters max).** Formula: what you are doing + what the product does + the ask. Example: `Solo founder. Building @brandgoblinai in public: type a business idea, my goblin Nix builds the whole brand in 2 min. Day X of 100 users in 30 days.` Adjust to fit. ([Postory](https://postory.io/blog/twitter-bio-ideas))
5. **Website field:** `https://brandgoblinai.com/?ref=x`. The bio link is not algorithm-punished (only links inside posts are), and it is your primary conversion path. The ?ref=x tag is your tracking tag from the acquisition plan, so signups get credited to X.
6. **Buy Premium ($8 tier)** per the section 1 verdict. Do it before you start replying, because reply priority is the feature you are actually buying.
7. **Pinned post:** on Day 6 this is your challenge kickoff post (section 5, post 1). After the challenge, it becomes a screenshot demo post with proof. Never leave the pin empty; a good post sends strangers to your profile, and the bio plus pinned post are what convert that visit into a follow or a click. ([OpenTweet](https://opentweet.io/blog/twitter-for-saas-founders-marketing-guide))
8. **Warm up for 3 to 5 days before posting** (this overlaps Days 1 to 5 of the master plan, before X officially starts on Day 6). Follow 50 to 100 accounts in your niche (list in section 6), turn on notifications for 10 to 15 of them, like and read genuinely, leave a handful of real replies. This trains the For You feed to show you the right rooms and trains your eye for what works. New accounts that instantly blast posts and mass-follow behave like bots and get treated like bots. ([Medium, Logan Holdsworth](https://medium.com/@loganholdsworth/a-full-guide-to-early-x-account-growth-8f3aebabe419))

## 4. FOUNDER OR CHARACTER: THE VERDICT

The question: should the account post AS Nix (a character account, where every post is written in the goblin's voice) or as Fox, the human founder building Nix?

**Verdict: post as Fox, the founder. Nix is your co-star, not your narrator.**

The reasoning, honestly:

1. **Character accounts work, but they are a comedy writing job.** Wendy's and Steak-umm proved a brand account with a strong personality can win X; Steak-umm went from 4,600 to 36,000 followers in about a year. But dig into every case and you find a dedicated human writer producing sharp, topical comedy daily, with full creative freedom. Duolingo's mascot empire is run by a social media team, and its wins came on TikTok video, not X text. You are one person with 30 minutes a day for this platform. A character account at that time budget produces weak bits, and a weak character account is worse than none. ([MEL Magazine](https://melmagazine.com/en-us/story/the-27-year-old-guy-who-tweets-for-steak-umm-is-millennial-angst-personified), [Marketing Case Studies](https://mynameiskalam.com/case-studies/wendys-twitter-strategy/), [Brand24](https://brand24.com/blog/duolingo-social-media-strategy/))
2. **The reply strategy breaks in character.** Your growth engine on X is replying usefully under founder and business accounts (section 6). A goblin leaving branding advice under a serious founder's post is confusing at best, mute-worthy at worst. Fox leaving sharp advice, with a goblin avatar that makes people curious, works.
3. **Build-in-public requires a human.** The challenge scoreboard, the honest numbers, the failure posts, all of it only lands because a real person is risking real embarrassment. A mascot cannot be vulnerable. The whole genre is founder-shaped.
4. **How Nix shows up anyway:** the profile photo, the screenshots (every brand kit demo has Nix in the product), an occasional post written as a one-line "Nix says" gag, and any video you cross-post from TikTok where you voice him. People will meet Nix constantly. He is the product's face; you are the account's voice. If the account someday earns a big audience and you want a dedicated @NixTheGoblin character account as a spinoff, that is a month 6 luxury, not a month 1 decision.

## 5. CONTENT FORMATS RANKED FOR BRANDGOBLIN, WITH READY-TO-POST EXAMPLES

Format facts from the research, then the posts. A "thread" is a chain of connected posts read in sequence. A "quote post" is resharing someone's post with your own comment on top. Key findings: video earns up to 10x the engagement of plain text, polls get 2 to 3x, images and screenshots reliably beat bare text, threads are declining because the algorithm now favors single longer posts (a Premium perk), and quote posts are treated as original content so they reach further than replies. The strongest text patterns: a confident one-sided opinion, a specific number in the first line, and the small observation that makes people feel seen. First 8 to 12 words decide everything. ([SocialBee](https://socialbee.com/blog/twitter-algorithm/), [PostEverywhere](https://posteverywhere.ai/blog/x-twitter-trends-2026), [Postory](https://postory.io/blog/what-goes-viral-on-twitter), [SuperX](https://superx.so/blog/what-is-a-quote-tweet))

**The ranking for you:**
1. **Scoreboard posts** (the challenge updates): text plus a simple numbers screenshot. Your signature format.
2. **Screenshot demos:** absurd idea in, brand kit out, posted as an image set with a short setup line. Nearly free, since TikTok batch day already generates these.
3. **The reply-with-an-idea game:** your comment-loop engine, adapted to X.
4. **Native video:** your best TikTok demos re-uploaded directly to X (upload the clean CapCut export, never a TikTok-watermarked file). One or two a week, no extra production cost.
5. **Opinion and observation posts:** short text takes on branding, naming, and looking legit on no budget. These build the follow, not the click.
6. **Polls:** cheap engagement, one a week.
7. **Threads:** skip them this month, the data says single posts now travel better, and threads eat writing time you do not have.

## 6. THE REPLY STRATEGY, PRECISELY

This is your actual growth engine. "Reply-guy strategy" is X slang for growing by leaving smart replies under big accounts so their audience discovers you. It earned a bad name from people leaving "Great post! 🔥" spam. Done right, it is simply being the most useful person in the comments. The documented zero-to-560k-impressions case ran almost entirely on this. ([IndieRadar](https://indieradar.app/blog/reply-guy-method-grow-x-twitter-zero-followers), [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/how-i-got-560k-impressions-on-x-in-10-days-with-zero-followers-and-zero-ad-spend-33c1e4d720))

**Who to follow (build this list during warm-up):**
- 15 to 20 big indie founder and build-in-public accounts (the people whose posts get hundreds of replies: think Pieter Levels, Marc Lou, Arvid Kahl, Justin Welsh, and whoever the current equivalents are when you search "build in public" and sort by engagement).
- 15 to 20 small business, side hustle, Etsy, and e-commerce voices (closer to your actual buyers).
- 10 to 15 branding, naming, design, and marketing accounts.
- 20 to 30 fellow small founders at your stage (these become mutuals, the people who reply to YOUR posts in the crucial first hour).
- Turn on the notification bell for the 10 to 15 whose posts reliably blow up, because reply speed matters: replies landing in the first 30 to 60 minutes of a big post ride its wave.

**How to find the right threads daily:** open notifications for your bell list, check the Following tab, and search terms your buyers use ("business idea," "naming my business," "logo cost," "starting an Etsy shop") sorted by Latest. Reply where you genuinely know something.

**What a good reply is:** specific to that exact post, adds something (an experience, a number, a counterpoint, a useful tool mention only when directly relevant), and short. Never open with praise, never paste your link unprompted, never reply the same words twice (copy-paste replies are a documented suppression trigger). ([Build Solo](https://buildsolo.io/how-to-reply-twitter-x/), [IPFoxy](https://www.ipfoxy.com/blog/ideas-inspiration/4577))

**Three worked examples:**

*Example 1. A big founder posts: "Spent $4k on branding before writing a line of code. Biggest early mistake."*
> Weak reply: "So true! Branding can wait 🔥"
> Good reply: "The trap is that 'looking real' feels like progress. I burned weeks on my first project polishing a logo for a product with zero users. Now I timebox identity to one day, ship, and only invest in brand once strangers actually show up. The $4k version isn't better, it's just slower."
(Why it works: personal specifics, a usable rule, zero pitch. People who like it click the goblin avatar, land on your bio, and the bio does the selling.)

*Example 2. A small business account asks: "How do you all come up with business names? I've been stuck for two weeks."*
> Good reply: "A working rule that unstuck me: say what it does plus one word of personality, then say it out loud in the sentence 'welcome to ___.' If you stumble, cut a syllable. Two weeks of stuck usually means you're searching for perfect instead of testable. Happy to throw a few at your idea if you share it."
(If they share the idea, generate options with BrandGoblin, post the best ones as screenshots, and say what you used. That is a product demo somebody asked for, which is the only kind that converts.)

*Example 3. A big account posts: "Unpopular opinion: most side hustles fail at marketing, not product."*
> Good reply: "Half agree. From watching new sellers this month: they fail at the step before marketing, looking credible enough for the first stranger to trust them. No name, mismatched visuals, no story. Marketing amplifies trust; you can't amplify zero. Fix the 'is this real?' problem first and mediocre marketing suddenly works."
(A respectful pushback with a fresh frame. Replies that disagree with substance start conversations, and conversations are the highest-weighted signal on the platform.)

**Daily quota: 10 to 15 real replies, 15 to 20 minutes.** The guides pushing 50 to 100 replies a day are describing spam with extra steps; volume without substance now gets downvoted and suppressed. Ten sharp replies under the right posts beat fifty generic ones, and the data backs quality: valuable replies drive the profile visits that convert to follows. ([Graham Mann](https://grahammann.net/blog/how-to-grow-on-x-twitter-2026), [Conbersa](https://www.conbersa.ai/learn/how-to-grow-twitter-from-zero))

## 7. THE REPEATABLE WEEKLY SYSTEM (HONEST TIME BUDGET)

X is your light channel. Here is the honest minimum that still works, and it fits inside the master plan without touching TikTok's time.

**Daily, 30 to 40 minutes, ideally split into two 15-to-20 minute sittings (morning and midday, when X is busiest):**
1. Post the day's scheduled post, or confirm the scheduler did (2 minutes).
2. Reply to everyone who replied to you, fast. First-hour conversation on your own posts is what makes the algorithm spread them (5 to 10 minutes).
3. The reply quota: 10 to 15 quality replies under your bell list and search finds (15 to 20 minutes).
4. Log X's line on the master plan's daily scorecard (1 minute).

**Weekly, on the existing Sunday batch day, add 45 to 60 minutes for X:**
- Write the week's 5 to 7 posts from the section 5 bank (scoreboard update, 2 to 3 screenshot demos, 1 opinion or observation, 1 poll or idea game, 1 video repost).
- Schedule them. Free options: X's own built-in scheduler on desktop (free, no third-party tool needed) or Buffer's free plan (10 scheduled posts per channel). Buffer is the pick if you want one dashboard; native is fine if you want zero new tools. ([OpenTweet](https://opentweet.io/free-twitter-scheduler), [PostEverywhere](https://posteverywhere.ai/blog/best-x-twitter-scheduler))
- Screenshots for demo posts come free from the TikTok batch session, same brand kits, zero extra generation work.

**Total: roughly 4 to 5 hours a week.** Below about 3 hours a week the reply engine stalls and the account produces nothing; be honest with yourself and either commit to the 30 daily minutes or park the channel entirely rather than half-run it. Posting frequency target: 1 original post a day, 5 to 7 a week, which matches the founder-account sweet spot in the 2026 data. More original posts than that is wasted on a small account; the marginal time always goes to replies instead. ([Monolit](https://monolit.sh/blog/how-many-times-a-week-should-you-post-on-twitter-x-2026-data-backed-answer-founders), [Tweet Archivist](https://www.tweetarchivist.com/twitter-posting-frequency-guide-2025))

## 8. TURNING ATTENTION INTO SIGNUPS

The funnel: reply or post, profile visit, bio or pinned post, link click, landing page, signup. Every step leaks. Your controls:

1. **The bio link is the workhorse.** brandgoblinai.com/?ref=x lives there permanently, unpunished. Most of your X signups will come from profile visits, not post links. That is why every reply is secretly an ad for your profile.
2. **Links in posts: first reply only.** When a post needs a link (challenge posts, PH day, demo posts), the main post carries no link and the first reply says "Free, no card, if you want to poke at it: [link]." This dodges the 30 to 50 percent link penalty on the post itself while keeping the link one tap away. ([Buffer](https://buffer.com/resources/links-on-x/))
3. **The pinned post carries proof.** Bio makes the promise, pin shows the evidence (the challenge post with real numbers, later a demo with screenshots). A profile visit that sees promise plus proof converts; a bare profile leaks. ([OpenTweet](https://opentweet.io/blog/twitter-for-saas-founders-marketing-guide))
4. **Say "free, no card" constantly.** It is your strongest line on every platform and it survives X's ad-smell allergy because it is just true.
5. **Realistic numbers, restated:** 30,000 to 80,000 monthly impressions with solid execution, 0.5 to 1.5 percent of link-adjacent impressions becoming clicks, 100 to 400 site visits, 5 to 15 signups. If the scoreboard story catches even modestly (one 100k-impression post), the month can double. Track it with the ?ref=x tag and judge the channel on data at Day 30, per the master plan's weekly review loop. ([Webeyez](https://webeyez.com/insights/guides/average-click-through-rate-twitter))

## 9. MISTAKES THAT KILL ACCOUNTS (THE BLUNT LIST)

1. **Putting links in every post.** The one behavior X most reliably buries, and repeated link-dropping is a documented suppression trigger. Bio and first reply, always. ([IPFoxy](https://www.ipfoxy.com/blog/ideas-inspiration/4577))
2. **Acting like a bot on a new account.** Follow bursts, rapid-fire likes, 30 replies in 10 minutes, posting 10 times on day one. New accounts get less benefit of the doubt, and automated restrictions kick in fast. Warm up slow. ([Multilogin](https://multilogin.com/blog/twitter-shadow-bans/))
3. **Copy-paste and AI-sloppy replies.** Identical short replies are a flag, and since March 2026 spammy or AI-flagged replies can be downvoted by Premium users, directly sinking your reach. Every reply gets written for that post or not at all. ([AutoTweet](https://www.autotweet.io/blog/x-shadowban-how-to-check-recover-2026), [AdLibrary](https://adlibrary.com/guides/x-twitter-algorithm-explained))
4. **Engagement bait.** "Repost if," "like if you agree," rage-bait questions, fake controversy. Short-term points, long-term mutes, and mutes and blocks now reduce your distribution to everyone. ([SocialBee](https://socialbee.com/blog/twitter-algorithm/))
5. **Pitching in other people's comments.** Dropping your product link under someone's post uninvited is the fastest route to blocks. Mention the product only when someone asks or the thread is literally about your exact problem space, and even then, name it without linking.
6. **Buying followers or using auto-engagement tools.** X's February 2026 crackdown suspends the account and associated accounts. Not a slap, a death sentence. ([Fireply](https://fireply.ai/blog/x-reply-bots-2026))
7. **Posting into the void and concluding X is dead.** Zero-follower accounts that only publish original posts get 20 views and quit. The channel is replies first, posts second, for the entire first month.
8. **Hashtags.** They do roughly nothing on X in 2026 and make posts look like spam. Zero hashtags. ([Postory](https://postory.io/blog/twitter-hashtags-2026))
9. **Arguing with strangers and dunking.** The algorithm will happily amplify your worst moment. Every public fight costs mutes from the quiet majority. Disagree with substance or scroll on.
10. **Judging the channel by week 2.** Reply-driven growth compounds slowly, then lumps. The honest evaluation point is Day 30 with the scorecard, same rule as every other channel in the master plan.
11. **Letting X eat TikTok's time.** The moment X's daily routine creeps past 45 minutes, it is stealing from the channel with the better math. The calendar below is capped on purpose.

## 11. WHAT TO AUTOMATE OR AI-ASSIST, AND WHAT NEVER TO

Worth it:
- **Drafting posts:** AI can draft variations of the section 5 templates and tighten your first lines. You do the final pass so everything sounds like Fox. Biggest legitimate saver.
- **Scheduling:** the free schedulers above are officially supported and safe. Queue the week on Sunday. ([OpenTweet](https://opentweet.io/blog/twitter-automation-rules-2026))
- **Screenshot prep:** brand kits from TikTok batch day cropped into X image sets, a mechanical job.
- **A swipe file:** when a post or reply of yours overperforms, save it. Month 2 runs on remixing your own winners.

Never:
- **Auto-replies or AI-written replies posted unreviewed.** Replies are your entire growth engine, reply quality is now a ranking signal, humans downvote obvious AI, and X's 2026 crackdown suspends accounts where "a human is not tapping the screen." This is the one place automation can kill the account outright. ([Fireply](https://fireply.ai/blog/x-reply-bots-2026), [Spider AF](https://spideraf.com/articles/twitter-bot-problem-how-bad-is-it-for-businesses))
- **Auto-follow, auto-like, auto-DM tools.** Documented suspension triggers, no exceptions.
- **The scoreboard numbers.** Never let AI round, smooth, or improve a number. The entire value of the challenge is that every figure is real. One caught fake ends the story for good.
- Rule of thumb, same as TikTok: automate the paperwork around the content, never the human inside it.
